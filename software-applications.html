Notable Classic Software Applications

  • Microsoft Office 97

    Microsoft Office 97 was a revolutionary productivity suite that introduced several new features, including the Office Assistant, better formatting tools, and expanded support for macros and templates. It became the industry standard for word processing, spreadsheets, and presentations, cementing Microsoft Office as the go-to suite for business and education.

  • Adobe Photoshop 4.0

    Adobe Photoshop 4.0, released in 1996, brought a new level of sophistication to image editing. It introduced layer effects, editable type, and a variety of new tools that made complex photo manipulation more accessible to users. Photoshop 4.0 is often seen as a landmark release in the evolution of digital imaging software.

  • Netscape Navigator

    Netscape Navigator was one of the first widely-used web browsers, dominating the early days of the internet in the mid-1990s. With features like bookmarks and tabbed browsing, it was instrumental in shaping the way people explored the web. Netscape's innovations laid the groundwork for modern browsers like Firefox and Chrome.

  • Winamp

    Winamp was a highly popular media player for Windows, known for its customizable skins and plug-ins. It was the go-to application for playing MP3 files during the late 1990s and early 2000s. Winamp also introduced users to visualizations and playlists, setting the standard for music playback software of the time.

  • Lotus 1-2-3

    Lotus 1-2-3 was a groundbreaking spreadsheet program released in 1983, becoming the killer app for IBM PCs. Its intuitive interface and advanced calculation capabilities made it the leading spreadsheet software for many years. Though eventually overtaken by Microsoft Excel, Lotus 1-2-3 was key in popularizing personal computing in businesses worldwide.
